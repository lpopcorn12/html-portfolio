About Me

I am 31 years old, I currently live in California and I am starting my new career as an Electrician as well as my side hobby now as a web developer where I can learn and improve my coding and hopefully design and build my own websites.

I have spent the last 8 years working as a Commercial Diver, which most people automatically think that all i ever do is weld underwater because they saw a few youtube videos of people doing it and to be honest that is what drove me to become a commercial diver in the first place as well, but in fact i have probably only welded twice in my 8 years in the field. Instead what i did the most of is Salvage, Burning, and Inspections. Over the years I have traveled to and worked in 19 or so different states (some of those states were just some Potable Water Tank cleaning and inspecting jobs that usually only took a few days so I don't know if I would count some of them) and done some pretty amazing things along the way. I have spent two months in panama city beach Florida following hurricane Micheal salvaging a semi-complete fishing vessel that was suppose to set sail the following year.

I spent almost a year and a half salvaging and scraping around 10 derelict vessels in a section of the Columbia River in Washington State that had been polluting the river for years

My spring and summer of 2019 was spent in a small town about 10 miles from superman's own town(Metropolis, IL) called Olmsted IL where I helped deconstruct an old lock and dam and also help with the construction of the new lock and dam that was meant to replace the old one. I will say that spending around 3 hours a day(or night in my case since I was on the night shift) underwater with a torch that can melt steel was pretty awesome and I was getting paid well which was a major plus.

The last year and a half of my diving career I spent traveling to every state west of the Mississippi River and I would climb, clean, and then inspect Potable water tanks(Mainly the drinking water for the cities that we traveled to). Some of the tanks would be 200' towers that you see driving on the freeway usually with the town name on it and those tanks we a pain to climb up.
